In sum, after looking through all the empirical materials and methods we’ve used from week2 to week7 , our group came up with two research questions:

  1. What is the discursive constitution of ‘fake news’?
  2. What is the relation between the construction of credibility and the consumption of ‘fake news’?

The first research question aim to explore the spectrum of fake-ness in ‘fake news’ and complexity of the notion of fake-ness in the media landscape. The second research question will focus on the credibility of ‘fake news’ through its convincing presentation and appearance in the media.

We have picked three different texts that are some of the most relevant for out research project and they are the following:

1. Brass Eye (1997) Clive Anderson shot dead. Available at: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=EGtdsXhXiEM [Accessed : 6 February, 2017]

This text is an video taken from a satire television program, Brass Eye. If the audience is aware that the programme is satire, they watch the show for the entertainment whereas the people who are unaware of the satire nature of the show, they will take the content of the program seriously.

2. Phipps, C. ‘Kellyanne Conway Blames Refugees for ‘Bowling Green Massacre’ That Never Happened.” The Guardian, Available at:  https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2017/feb/03/kellyanne-conway-refugees-bowling-green-massacre-never-happened. [Accessed: February 3, 2017]

This text is a video of the news story showing Kellyanne Conway giving an interview about the ‘Bowling Green Massacre,’ a terrorist attack that never happened. Without fact-checkers, the public will never find out about the inaccuracy of the news report.

3. Anthony, S. ‘Was the 2013 World Press Photo of the Year faked with Photoshop, or merely   manipulated?’ https://www.extremetech.com/extreme/155617-how-the-2013-world-press-photo-of-the-year-was-faked-with-photoshop. [Accessed: 10 February, 2017]

This text is an example of an award-winning photograph that was exposed as being doctored, this shows that with the advance in technology, sometimes the truth is not as simple and as obvious as what we see in the media.

Collectively, all of these texts showed a varying  degree of fake-ness within ‘fake news’ and demonstrate that ‘fake news’ is a global issue that pre-dates the 2016 US Presidential Election.
