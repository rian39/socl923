Week 7   Live methods Analysis on Design of Apple Website From 1996 to 2016

Link 

https://web.archive.org/web/*/www.apple.com/

Background Information

This week our group runs an Internet Archive Wayback Machine search on Apple website (www.apple.com) from 1996 to 2016 involving live methods. We divide the 20 years into 2 periods and use semiotic and textural analysis to make it easier and more clear for understanding.

Live Methods Analysis

Routine design: 1996 – 1998

Figure 1: 1997

    In 1996, Apple complied with the trend to design the website like an E-mail which shows the styled site with the banner, search bar, accessibility information, navigation, and sidebar. However, 2 years later, Apple renewed the website from a site to provide information of the company into a platform to introduce products. The design of 1998 laid the foundation for that in the following 15 years – highlighted 4 characteristics including a big sector in the middle of website which exhibited key products, few small sectors at the bottom which contained hot news, two or three sectors of secondary products, and a navigation bar at the top of the screen.

Figure 2: 1998

Style design: 1998 – 2016

The website stayed as a product of Apple as well as a service to promote products, changing with the times and new information. We divide the whole page into four parts –from the top to the bottom, the design follows Pyramid Principles to put key words/introduction on the top and bottom and search engine in the middle. In figure 2, it shows ‘volume’. In the design, part 1 highlighted the product to attract readers. The design in 1998 lay the foundation of that in the following 19 years. Since then it is important to discuss how to improve 4 parts to adapt to suit consumers’ tastes.

Figure 3: 2001

    Apple has always had a special relationship with the element of water (Aqua). In 2001, navigation bar and hot news sectors shared features of Aqua − such designs are often quite complex, include rich imagery and vivid animation effects.

Figure 4: 2008

    The space of the big sector kept expanding since 1998. In 2016, in order to promote new Iphone 6, the sector is double that of 1998. On the contrary, sectors of hot news became smaller and smaller and finally blended into the background in 2008. This change related to Apple’s brand awareness. In the 2000s, the official website transformed into a sales platform because Apple was so famous that it did not need to introduce company cultures specifically. And sectors of secondary became independent by adding outline border shadow.

Figure 5: 2016

Conclusion

Apple focuses on details. In 20 years, like Imac and Iphone, its website revised and reorganized details, combining art, function, and self-created culture. We conclude main changes as following after viewing designs in 20 years: a) the simple design highlights core product and avoids secondary data accordingly; b) the clear navigation bar make users feel free to browse products; c) the same font in different sizes differentiate ‘volume’ of information; and d) the appropriate color matching, light and shadow make users feel comfortable. The process of changing is from ‘more’ to ‘less’.

In today’s discussion, our group describe initial observation of Apple by analyzing snapshots of their website, work on digital ethnography, and summarize the design path to success.
