Week 6 Ethnography: studying what people do with media

On March 3, 2017March 19, 2017 By Team 1

Imagining field sites

● List three specific places that you could study (given a few weeks!) as ethnographic field sites for your group research project

  1. Online Douban group:

https://www.douban.com/group/144218/

This is an online group called “Onion Time” on Douban website which will post every side of fake news reported in social media everyday. All of these fake news are made up by the group members. The original events are from daily life, happening among us. Being processed and fabricated they will become fake news, spreading widely for amusement.

2.  CCTV (China Central Television)

http://www.cctv.com/index.shtml       (or on TV directly)

CCTV is the biggest television station and the predominant state television broadcaster in China. Most of its programmes are a mixture of news, documentary, social education, comedy and entertainment.

3.  Kitchen (on meal time)

When we are cooking or eating meals with roomies in the same flat we share our ideas about news we are watching. There will always be a furious discussion on the interesting part of it, including fake news, which attracts us mostly.

● List some of the people you would want to meet, talk to and spend time with in these places.

  1. The group members. But unfortunately this group was seemed to be abandoned and there was no update since a long time ago. We can still see the previous work they did, maybe talk to the members about the reason why they did not insist to work on it.
  2. It’s a kind of indirect communication and  direct reception of information on TV.
  3. Roomie, friends and anyone we can meet in our kitchen.

● List some of the things, artefacts, practices or events you would want to observe or participate in as an ethnographer.

[ Hillary Clinton Sick with Pneumonia ]

http://www.livetradingnews.com/hillary-clinton-pneumonia-14947.html#.WMJekoXXJPb

Hillary Clinton was diagnosed with pneumonia Friday, her doctor said in a statement Sunday after the Democratic nominee left the 9/11 Remembrance Memorial ceremony at Ground Zero due to what her campaign said was “overheating”.

“Secretary Clinton has been experiencing a cough related to allergies. On Friday, during follow up evaluation of her prolonged cough, she was diagnosed with pneumonia,” her doctor, Lisa Bardack, said.

“She was put on antibiotics, and advised to rest and modify her schedule. While at this morning’s event, she became overheated and dehydrated. I have just examined her and she is now re-hydrated and recovering nicely.”

Video posted by a bystander to Twitter appeared to show Mrs. Clinton extremely unsteady and supported by aides, being helped from the curb into a vehicle.

● How would you address the problems of access to sub-cultures, elites, niche social movements or marginal/vulnerable groups?

  1. There will be some technical problems like group is close so we can not join in it; group was closed (had met) so the information and news are out of date; seldom update of articles and members are not active. -Try to find some opened and active groups.
  2. The news on TV are all real news and our countries will announce  that the fake news is unreal. -They care about influence in media and we should make our choice.
  3. We are always easy to chat on something not related to the fake news topic when we are have s discussion. -More focus and concentrate. Be serious.
