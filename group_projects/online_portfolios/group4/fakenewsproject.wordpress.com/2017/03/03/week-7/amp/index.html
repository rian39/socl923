Following Baudrillard, fake news could be read as a symptom generated by the hyper realâ€¦ Hyper-reality being a world in which the lack of the real, is covered up by a paranoiac over production of simulacra (signs that do not seek to represent the real) (1981). Baudrillard raises an interesting point regarding Disney land. Disney land functions in so far as it pretends to be outside of normality, a fictional world (1981). In this way it covers up the fact the wider society is just as hyper real. Perhaps this logic could be applied to fake news, as hyper reality dissolves oppositions such as true and false. Perhaps fake news can be read as sustaining the illusion that the opposition between true and false is a meaningful distinction in a world saturated by simulacra. Additionally, following the article that reads fake news through Spinoza, this reading may provide a critique of fake news that does not fall into the largely meaningless debate concerning the truth. Events such as climate change, the election of Donald trump (a proven liar) and the proliferation of fake news suggests we cant appose untruth with truth alone. But again following the SpinozaÂ text, the affects fake news produces must be and can be challenged by alternative affects. Also I suggest caution with the Baudrilardian reading in so far as we can not give up on the distinction between true and false entirely, as fake news is predicated on this opposition.

We can take two directions, either give up on the distinction between real and fake news, or consider Baudrillards theory as a partial one, a useful tool for challenging oppositions, but by no means a totalising theory. In other words reading Baudrillards theory of simulacra as documenting a process whereby more and more of reality becomes hyper real, which results in an increased disorientation regarding distinctions such as true and false, real and fake. But by no means totally forecloses the possibility of these categories having some validity. In this way perhaps the truth needs to be supplemented with affect, as time and time again it would appear affect is far more powerful than the notion of the truth in a post truth or hyper real society.
